How Does It Work?

Google Maps publicly displays the capture month and year for its panoramas, but the precise time of day is hidden deep within their backend systems. The Pano Date Extractor interacts directly with the hidden Google Maps API using a rapid binary search algorithm.

By repeatedly pinging the database with narrowing timestamp filters, the tool pinpoints the exact UNIX timestamp when the Google Street View car snapped the photograph. It then converts this raw data into the accurate local time of the location, automatically adjusting for historical Daylight Saving Time (DST) rules.
